The Eachine Wizard X220S is a 220mm FPV Racing quadcopter and it is the evolution of the X220; it comes both in RTF (Ready To Fly) and ARF (Almost Ready to Fly) version. If you already have a decent transmitter, you can just go for the ARF version and buy a receiver that uses the same protocol as the TX: this is exactly what we are going to see in this article.

Package contents

The ARF version package comes with the quadcopter with the following specifications and accessories:

  • 4 x Eachine MN2206 2300KV motor
  • Eachine 30A BLHELI_S 4 in 1 ESC
  • Eachine HS1179 800TVL CCD camera
  • 5.8 GHz 72CH 25MW 200mw 600MW switchable transmitter
  • Pagoda antenna 5.8 GHz
  • Omnibus F4 V3 flight controller
  • 10 x pairs of Eachine 5051 3-blade propeller
  • 30° Camera Mount (For Runcam3/Gopro session)
  • 2 x Battery strap
  • 2 x Wrench

TX, RX, LiPo Battery, Propellers

The ARF version does not come with TX, RX and battery, so here is my setup:

  • Devo 7e loaded with Deviation firmware and additional RF modules so it is possible to bind different models with just one radio controller. In this case, the RF module we are going to use is the A7105 (XL7105-D03B chip) which supports AFHDS-2A (FlySky protocol);
  • Flysky X6B 2.4G 6CH i-BUS PPM PWM Receiver for AFHDS;
  • TATTU 1550mAh 14.8V 75C 4S1P Lipo Battery Pack;
  • DALPROP T5045C; changing propellers is a must since the given ones are really inefficient and the quality is not as good as it should be (they develop stress marks after few flights).

Receiver connection to FC board

In the receiver package, there are four cables: we need two of them, the one going inside the top right port for i-BUS and the one going in the bottom side port that we are going to use for battery voltage monitoring:

Both cables have three wires (black, red and yellow) with a white connector on one side (the one going into the receiver) and a black one on the other side:

For battery voltage monitoring we just need the red and black wires, so we need to remove the yellow one from one of the two cables. For both cables, the side with the black connector needs to be cut. Then we can strip the wires and prepare them for the soldering on the FC.

Now we need access to the top of the Flight Controller board. By doing this, we need to remove the top carbon plate (six screws) and the VTX board:

Mounting the receiver

Now we just find a good place to position the receiver. A safe spot could be on the rear, on the bottom plate near the leds. To fix the board on the carbon plate we can just use two M2 standoffs.

X6B Binding

To bind Flysky X6B receiver we need to turn on the quadcopter while pressing th “BIND” button on the receiver and using the binding mode on the TX. For Devo 7e, this can be done in Model menu -> Model setup.

Conclusions
